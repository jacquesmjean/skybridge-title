Glossary of title terms

Title
Legal proof that you own a property.
Title insurance
A one-time policy that protects you and your lender from past ownership problems, liens, or fraud.
Title search
A review of public records to confirm the seller can legally sell and the property is clear.
Escrow
A neutral account that holds money and documents until every condition of the sale is met.
Closing (settlement)
The final step where documents are signed, funds change hands, and ownership transfers to you.
Deed
The document that legally transfers ownership from the seller to the buyer.
Lien
A legal claim against a property for an unpaid debt — it must be cleared before a clean transfer.
Underwriter
The insurance company that backs your title policy financially.
Owner’s vs. lender’s policy
The owner’s policy protects you; the lender’s policy protects the bank’s loan.
Closing Disclosure
A form listing your final loan terms and every cost, given before you sign.
Documentary stamp tax
A Florida tax on the deed and mortgage, paid at closing.
Wire fraud
Scams that trick buyers into sending funds to a fake account — always verify wiring details by phone.

Frequently asked questions

What does a title company actually do?

We confirm the seller truly owns the property, clear any liens or claims, insure your ownership, hold funds safely in escrow, and handle the signing so your closing is smooth and protected.

Do I really need title insurance?

Yes. It is a one-time cost that protects your ownership for as long as you own the home — against hidden problems like unknown heirs, forged documents, or old unpaid debts.

Who pays for closing costs in Florida?

It is negotiable and varies by county. In most of South Florida the seller customarily pays for the owner’s title policy, but this can be set in the contract. Use our instant calculator for an itemized estimate.

How long does closing take?

A typical residential closing runs 2–4 weeks from contract to keys, depending on financing and the title search. Cash deals can close faster.

What do I bring to closing?

A government-issued photo ID and any funds due by wire or cashier’s check. We send a full checklist ahead of time so there are no surprises.

How do I protect myself from wire fraud?

Never trust wiring instructions sent by email alone. Always call our office at a known number to verify before sending any funds. Skybridge will never change wire details by email.

Guide

Short answer: we make sure the home you are buying is really yours to buy, with no hidden claims, debts, or ownership problems. We also handle the money and the paperwork on closing day, so everything is safe and legal.

We check the history of the property

Before you buy, we search public records to confirm who truly owns the home and whether anyone else has a claim on it. That could be an unpaid loan, a contractor lien, back taxes, or a boundary dispute. This is called a title search. If we find a problem, we clear it before you close.

We protect you with title insurance

Even a careful search can miss something hidden, like a forged signature or an unknown heir. Title insurance protects you and your lender if a problem shows up later. You pay once, at closing, and you are covered for as long as you own the home.

We handle closing day

We hold everyone's money safely in escrow, prepare and explain the documents, make sure the seller is paid and old loans are cleared, and record the new deed with the county. You leave with the keys and clear ownership.

Why it matters: buying a home is likely the biggest purchase of your life. A title company is the neutral third party that makes sure it is done right, so years from now no one can question that the home is yours.

Guide

Closing costs are the fees you pay once to finalize a home purchase, on top of your down payment. In Florida, they usually run about 2% to 5% of the purchase price. Here is what they cover, in plain language.

The main costs

  • Title insurance: protects your ownership. In Florida the rate is set by the state, so it is the same no matter which title company you choose.
  • Settlement or closing fee: what the title company charges to handle the closing.
  • Recording and transfer taxes: Florida charges documentary stamp taxes to record the deed and any mortgage.
  • Lender fees: if you use a mortgage, this covers origination, appraisal, and credit costs.
  • Prepaids: property taxes and homeowner's insurance paid ahead into an escrow account.

Who pays for what?

In most of Florida, the seller traditionally pays for the owner's title insurance policy and their share of taxes, while the buyer pays the lender fees and their own prepaids. But everything is negotiable and can be written into the contract. In Miami-Dade County the custom can differ, and we will tell you exactly what applies to your deal.

No surprises

Before closing, you receive a Closing Disclosure that lists every dollar. We walk through it with you line by line, in your language, so nothing is a mystery.

Guide

Buying your first home is exciting, and a little overwhelming. Here is a simple checklist to have ready, so your closing goes smoothly.

Before you shop

  • Check your credit and know your budget.
  • Get preapproved by a lender so you know what you can borrow.
  • Save for your down payment plus closing costs, about 2% to 5% of the price.

When you find the home

  • Make an offer with your realtor and sign the purchase contract.
  • Send the contract to us. We open your title order and start the title search.
  • Schedule your home inspection.

Before closing day

  • Have a photo ID from the government ready for everyone signing.
  • Arrange your funds by wire. Never send money without confirming the instructions with us directly by phone.
  • Do a final walkthrough of the home.
  • Review your Closing Disclosure. We explain every line.

Closing day

You sign the documents, we handle the money and record your deed, and you get the keys. That is it. You are a homeowner.

A note on safety: wire fraud is common in real estate. Skybridge will never change payment instructions by email. Always call us at the number on our site to confirm before sending any funds.
